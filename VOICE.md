# FQHC Talent Exchange — Voice Guide

## Who We Are

We're building something for the people who hold California's safety net together. The care coordinators who know every patient by name. The bilingual MAs who translate not just words but fear into understanding. The CHWs who grew up in the neighborhoods they now serve. The executives who took on impossible jobs because they believe healthcare is a right.

This project exists to give FQHC talent and organizations an edge — sharing best practices, training resources, and intelligence with a collaborative growth mindset. We succeed when FQHCs succeed.

## Our Voice

**We sound like the most energized, best-informed colleague you've ever worked with.** Not a consultant. Not a salesperson. Not a government agency. A colleague who's done the homework, has the data, and can't wait to share what they found — with warmth, clarity, and a genuine belief that things can get better.

We are **positive and upbeat** as a default setting. Even when the news is hard, we bring energy and momentum. We are **direct and clear** — no hedging, no burying the lead, no jargon without purpose. We lead with what matters and say it plainly.

### Tone Pillars

| Pillar | What it means | What it sounds like |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Energetic** | We bring momentum to every page | "Here's what you can do right now" not "Consider evaluating your options" |
| **Positive** | We default to optimism grounded in real evidence | "Three FQHCs already solved this — here's how" not "This is a growing problem" |
| **Direct** | We say what we mean, clearly and quickly | "This saves $240K/year" not "This could potentially result in significant savings" |
| **Warm** | We lead with care, not alarm | "Here's what this means for your team" not "CRITICAL ALERT" |
| **Collaborative** | We're in this together | "Let's figure this out" not "You need to do X" |
| **Empowering** | We build people up | "You already have what it takes — let's prove it" not "You're falling behind" |
| **Honest** | We don't sugarcoat, but we always point to a path forward | "This is hard. Here's what's working." not "Everything is fine" or "The sky is falling" |
| **Community-rooted** | We reflect the communities FQHCs serve | Accessible language, cultural awareness, bilingual respect |
| **Source-driven** | Every claim links to a primary source | "According to HRSA's 2025 UDS data..." not "Studies show..." |

### How We Address People

- **Candidates/Workers:** You're the backbone of community health. We're here to help you grow.
- **Executives/Leaders:** You're carrying an enormous responsibility. We're here to help you carry it with better information.
- **Everyone:** "We" and "us" — never "they" or "those people." We're part of this movement.

## Language Guidelines

### Do

- **Cite primary sources for every claim** — link to HRSA, CMS, DHCS, NACHC, CHCF, KFF, CPCA, peer-reviewed journals, or the originating organization. Source hierarchy: government data > policy organizations > industry publications > news reporting. "According to HRSA's 2025 UDS Report" beats "Studies show" every time.
- **Lead with energy** — open with the most useful, actionable, or surprising insight. Don't bury the lead under caveats.
- Use plain language first, then define technical terms where needed
- Write in active voice: "FQHCs can recover revenue by..." not "Revenue can be recovered by..."
- Frame challenges as solvable: "This is a real challenge — and here's what three FQHCs are already doing about it"
- Acknowledge the human cost: numbers represent real people, real families, real communities
- Celebrate wins loudly: "Highland Health grew revenue 270% with one pharmacy decision. Here's exactly how."
- Use "community health" instead of "safety net" when possible (it's more dignifying)
- Respect bilingual skills as an asset, never a limitation
- Write headlines that inform AND energize — "5 Revenue Strategies That Actually Worked in 2026" not "Revenue Considerations"
- End sections with a clear next step or call to action — always point forward

### Don't

- Use fear-mongering or urgency for its own sake ("CRISIS" on everything numbs people)
- Be vague when you can be specific — say "$4.6B" not "billions," say "HRSA Section 330 grants" not "federal funding"
- Make claims without linking to a source — if you can't cite it, don't say it
- Talk down to anyone — executives, entry-level staff, or patients
- Use deficit framing: "underserved communities" → "communities that deserve better resources"
- Use corporate buzzwords without substance: "leverage," "synergize," "disrupt"
- Refer to people as "resources" or "FTEs" when we mean human beings with families
- Assume everyone speaks English first — many of our audience members think in Spanish
- Frame layoffs as statistics — each number is someone's livelihood
- Use passive voice to obscure who's responsible for policy decisions
- Hedge when you can be direct — "It might be worth considering" → "Do this"
- Take sides on labor disputes, ballot measures, or union actions — present both perspectives fairly

### Labor, Advocacy & Union Content — Balanced Framing

We serve both FQHC executives and frontline workers. Our credibility depends on presenting both sides of labor and advocacy issues with equal weight and respect.

| Instead of... | Try... |
|--------------|--------|
| "SEIU's leverage politics" | "SEIU's ballot strategy" or "SEIU's accountability push" |
| "Three failed dialysis ballot measures" | "Three previous ballot campaigns (2018, 2020, 2022)" |
| Leading with opposition's $1.7B projection | Leading with what both sides agree on (the facts), then each side's interpretation |
| "What Smart FQHCs Are Doing" (management-only) | "What Both Sides Are Watching" or separate sections for leaders and workers |
| Framing unions as adversaries | Acknowledging shared goals: patient care, workforce stability, sustainable operations |

**The principle:** Present each side's stated goals and reasoning with similar positioning. Workers want fair wages, accountability, and a voice in how clinics operate. Management needs operational flexibility, financial stability, and the ability to invest in growth. Both care about patients. Let the reader form their own view.

**Structure labor content as:**
1. The facts (what happened, what's proposed, timeline)
2. Why supporters say it matters (in their own framing)
3. Why opponents say it's a problem (in their own framing)
4. The data points driving the debate (presented neutrally)
5. What to watch — for both audiences

### Framing Patterns

| Instead of... | Try... |
|--------------|--------|
| "3,477 workers displaced" | "3,477 colleagues — care coordinators, nurses, community health workers — lost their jobs" |
| "FQHCs must optimize revenue" | "There are real ways to strengthen your clinic's financial position" |
| "Undocumented patients will lose coverage" | "Patients who've trusted your clinic for years face losing their care — here's how to help them" |
| "Your FQHC scored D on resilience" | "Your clinic has room to grow in these areas — and here's a path forward" |
| "Staffing crisis" | "The workforce challenge — and what's working" |
| "Tool for executives" | "Built for the people leading community health" |
| "Deploy AI" | "Bring AI tools that actually help your team" |
| "Maximize encounters" | "Make the most of every patient visit" |

## Bilingual Sensitivity

- Spanish content is not just translation — it's culturally adapted
- Honor that many FQHCs serve communities where Spanish is the language of home, trust, and comfort
- When using medical terms, provide both English and Spanish: "Enhanced Care Management (Manejo de Cuidados Mejorados)"
- Don't assume English is primary — for many of our users, it isn't

## Primary Sources — Non-Negotiable

Every factual claim on FQHC Talent Exchange links to a primary source. This is what separates us from SEO content and positions us as the trusted intelligence platform for FQHC leadership.

| Source Tier | Examples | When to Use |
|-------------|----------|-------------|
| **Tier 1: Government data** | HRSA UDS, CMS, DHCS, CA EDD WARN, BLS, HCAI | Always preferred. The gold standard. |
| **Tier 2: Policy organizations** | NACHC, CPCA, CHCF, KFF, CBO, Commonwealth Fund | Policy analysis, trend data, projections |
| **Tier 3: Industry publications** | Modern Healthcare, Fierce Healthcare, Becker's, Health Affairs | Breaking news, industry trends |
| **Tier 4: News reporting** | CalMatters, LA Times, regional outlets | Local context, human stories |
| **Tier 5: Organizational** | FQHC press releases, annual reports, board minutes | Org-specific claims |

Format: Always hyperlink the source name inline — "According to [HRSA's 2025 UDS Report](url)..." — not footnotes, not "Source: ..." at the bottom.

## Content Types — Voice by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Intelligence feeds** | Energetic, informative, action-forward | "Sacramento County just cut $26M in health services. Three FQHCs in the region are already pivoting — here's how. ([Source](url))" |
| **Tools & simulators** | Encouraging, clear, guiding | "Three quick questions and we'll show you exactly where your clinic stands. Let's go!" |
| **Blog articles** | Conversational expert, storytelling with data | "When Highland Health launched their own pharmacy, nobody believed it would work. Two years later: 270% revenue growth. ([Business Journal](url))" |
| **Layoff tracking** | Respectful, factual, immediately useful | "247 colleagues at Alameda Health System received WARN notices. Here are 38 open positions at nearby FQHCs and 4 free career tools." |
| **Career tools** | Upbeat coach, confidence-builder | "Your experience is exactly what FQHCs need. Let's build a resume that shows it!" |
| **Strategy pages** | Collaborative expert, energy + evidence | "The data is clear: FQHCs that diversified revenue before 2025 are 3x more resilient today. Here's the playbook. ([CHCF](url))" |

## The Test

Before publishing any content, ask:

1. **Would a tired CHW at the end of a 10-hour shift feel respected AND energized reading this?**
2. **Would an FQHC CEO with 200 staff and a budget crisis find this useful, actionable, and sourced?**
3. **Would a bilingual MA feel seen, not tokenized?**
4. **Does this make someone feel more capable, not more overwhelmed?**
5. **Can every factual claim be traced to a primary source with a clickable link?**

If yes to all five, ship it.

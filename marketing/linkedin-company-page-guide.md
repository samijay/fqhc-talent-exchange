## 2. LinkedIn Newsletter (High Priority — Do This Week)

LinkedIn Newsletters are the single highest-leverage feature for your goal. When someone subscribes to your LinkedIn Newsletter, they get a push notification every time you publish. Average open rates: 40-60% (vs. 20% for email).

### How to Set It Up
1. Go to your Company Page → Admin View
2. Click "Write article" → You'll see an option to create a Newsletter
3. LinkedIn may require Super Admin access and the page to have 150+ followers first. If you're under 150, focus on growing followers first (see Section 4).

### Newsletter Name Options
- "FQHC Intel Brief" (matches your email newsletter brand)
- "California FQHC Monitor" (descriptive, searchable)
- "The Safety Net Signal" (distinctive, memorable)

### Newsletter Description
> Weekly intelligence for FQHC executives and healthcare professionals in California. Policy shifts, workforce data, AI adoption, and strategic analysis — sourced from primary data, not SEO content. Covers H.R. 1 impacts, Medi-Cal changes, layoff tracking, and resilience scoring for 214 FQHCs.

### Cadence
Weekly (Tuesday or Wednesday). This matches the research: nearly 60% of top-performing LinkedIn newsletters publish weekly. Monthly loses momentum, daily overwhelms.

### Content Strategy
Repurpose your existing Intel Brief email newsletter content. Each LinkedIn Newsletter issue should:
- Lead with one high-impact intel item (your "Crisis Intel" post, expanded)
- Include 3-4 bullet intel items with links to your site
- End with a CTA to subscribe to the full email newsletter for daily updates

This creates a funnel: LinkedIn Newsletter (weekly, broad reach) → Email Newsletter (weekly, deeper content) → Site (daily intel, tools, reports)

## 3. Posting Strategy

### Cadence
5 posts per week (Mon-Fri), one per day. The scheduled task generates these automatically each Monday morning. You review, tweak if needed, and publish.

### Post Types (Weekly Rotation)

Post between 8-10am Pacific. LinkedIn's B2B engagement peaks Tuesday-Thursday 8am-12pm, but consistency matters more than perfection.

### Hashtag Strategy (3-5 per post, always include first two)

- #CommunityHealth (broader reach)
- Then rotate: #MediCal, #HealthcareWorkforce, #HealthIT, #CaliforniaHealthcare, #FQHCLeadership, #PublicHealth, #SafetyNet, #HealthEquity

## 4. Growth Tactics (0 to 500 Followers)

### Phase 1: Seed Network (Week 1-2)
- **Invite your LinkedIn connections** to follow the page (LinkedIn allows 250 invites/month from each admin)
- **Add the page to your personal profile** as your current company
- **Share each company post** from your personal profile with a brief comment — personal profiles get 5-10x more reach than company pages
- **Ask 5-10 people** in your network to follow + engage on the first few posts

### Phase 2: Strategic Engagement (Ongoing)
- **Comment on CPCA posts** — they post regularly. Leave substantive comments (2-3 sentences with a data point) on their workforce and policy content. Don't link-drop, just demonstrate expertise. People click your profile → see FQHC Talent Exchange → follow.
- **Comment on NACHC, CHCF, KFF posts** — same approach.
- **Engage with FQHC HR directors** who post about hiring challenges. Comment with relevant data from your platform.
- **Find and follow these LinkedIn accounts:**
  - CPCA (@CaliforniaPrimaryCareAssociation)
  - NACHC (@NACHC)
  - CHCF (@CalHealthCare)
  - Kaiser Family Foundation (@KFF)
  - Individual FQHC executives (search "FQHC CEO California" or "FQHC HR Director")

### Phase 3: Content Amplification (Week 3+)
- **Cross-post your best-performing LinkedIn post** to the FQHC subreddit (r/publichealth, r/healthcareworkers)
- **Share your Intel Brief** in relevant Facebook groups (there are FQHC-specific groups)
- **Email signature** — add "Subscribe to the FQHC Intel Brief on LinkedIn" with a link

## 5. Content That Converts Followers to Newsletter Subscribers

The funnel: See post → Follow page → See newsletter → Subscribe → Visit site → Subscribe to email

Every post should have ONE of these conversion hooks:
- "FQHC Talent Exchange tracks this at [link]" (drives site traffic)
- "Subscribe to the Intel Brief for weekly analysis: [newsletter link]" (drives newsletter)
- "See the full data: [specific page link]" (drives deep engagement)

### What NOT to do
- Don't ask people to subscribe in every post (1 in 5 max)
- Don't use engagement bait ("agree?", polls about obvious things)
- Don't post generic healthcare content — your edge is California FQHC specificity
- Don't post more than once per day from the company page

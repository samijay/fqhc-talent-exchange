## POST 1: Crisis Intel (Monday, March 10)

**Headline:** California's PPS Elimination Crisis: July 2026 Deadline

**Body:**

FQHC Talent Exchange tracks a critical policy deadline that will reshape California's safety-net workforce in just 4 months.

Effective July 1, 2026, California will eliminate the Prospective Payment System (PPS) for patients in the Undocumented Immigrant Services (UIS) program. For many FQHCs serving immigrant communities, this represents a $1 billion sector-wide revenue impact.

What does this mean for your team?

FQHCs with 30%+ undocumented patient volume face sudden revenue pressure. This will force workforce decisions: hiring freezes, recruitment speed-ups for billable programs like ECM, or potential layoffs in administration and community health worker roles.

California and 23 other states have filed suit challenging these cuts — but the July 1 deadline remains in effect unless courts intervene. Preparation time: 112 days.

## POST 3: AI Watch (Wednesday, March 12)

**Headline:** The Ambient AI Ethics Question: Revenue Capture or Burnout Relief?

**Body:**

FQHC Talent Exchange monitors AI adoption across California's safety net. This week's critical finding: new evidence that ambient AI scribes are being deployed not just to reduce clinician burnout, but to capture revenue through more intensive coding.

A March 2026 policy brief in PMC documents the trend. At one health system, deploying an AI scribe increased physician wRVUs by 11% and HCC diagnoses per encounter by 14%. Good outcome for revenue? Or coding inflation?

For FQHCs, the answer matters differently than it does for traditional hospitals.

Under PPS reimbursement, most FQHCs can't "upcode" their way to more revenue — they get the same payment regardless of diagnosis coding intensity. But FQHCs in value-based contracts or Medicare Advantage capitation need to watch carefully. AI-inflated risk scores could trigger audits or fraud investigations.

The opportunity is real (eliminate "pajama time," reduce burnout, save 13 minutes per visit). The risk is also real.

FQHC Talent Exchange tracks AI adoption across the sector at https://www.fqhctalent.com/ai-tracker — including the latest on NACHC-eClinicalWorks partnerships, Elation Health + Claude, and the ethics frameworks emerging around ambient documentation.

## POST 4: Workforce Story (Thursday, March 13)

**Headline:** 3,477+ Healthcare Workers Displaced — The FQHC Share

**Body:**

California's healthcare safety net is in crisis.

FQHC Talent Exchange tracks confirmed workforce reductions across California. Since January 2026, 20 organizations have announced or executed layoffs affecting 3,477 workers.

11 of those 20 are FQHCs.

The human impact:

San Ysidro Health in San Diego — one of the nation's largest FQHCs serving 107,000+ patients — is laying off 75 community health workers and care coordinators. These are the staff who do the cultural navigation work, the follow-up calls, the patient advocacy.

Inland Empire Health Plan's restructuring affects 120 positions across contracted FQHC partners including Borrego Health and Desert Healthcare District.

LA County Department of Health Services is cutting 500 community health worker and care coordination roles — the positions that connect Medi-Cal patients to services.

These aren't just budget numbers. These are CHWs who know their communities, navigators who speak Khmer or Hmong or Spanish with fluency, care coordinators managing complex cases of seniors and undocumented immigrants.

The crisis is real. The opportunity is too: FQHCs need strategic workforce planning, not reactive cuts.

FQHC Talent Exchange tracks every layoff announcement with transition toolkit resources at https://www.fqhctalent.com/layoffs
